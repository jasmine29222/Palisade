Questions

Before you ask.

We tried automation before and it broke.

That's usually a maintenance problem, not an automation problem — most agencies build it and disappear. That's the entire reason the retainer exists: I'm the one who fixes it when your CRM changes its API six months from now.

Is this going to replace my staff?

It replaces the parts of the job people already hate — chasing leads, retyping the same information into three tools. That frees your team for the parts that actually need a person: closing the deal, handling the tricky client.

How do I know the AI won't say something embarrassing to a customer?

For anything client-facing, it drafts and a person approves before it sends — until you've seen enough of the pattern to trust it further. You decide exactly where that checkpoint sits.

How long does a build actually take?

Most single-workflow builds go live within 10 business days of kickoff. Multi-system builds run 3–4 weeks. You'll get a specific date on your proposal, not a range.

What if we outgrow the system or need something custom later?

That's what the quarterly strategy call on the Partner tier is for. Systems get extended as you grow — you're not locked into what was scoped on day one.
